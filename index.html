Strategic Funding Landscape

CSP Funding Strategy 2026

A strategic roadmap for the College of Society and Professions. Navigating key deadlines and securing flagship funding.

2026 Funding Calendar

The 2026 funding calendar shows two distinct periods of high activity. The Spring (Feb-Apr) is busy with outline deadlines, followed by a quieter Summer period. This Summer lull offers a convenient window to prepare for the major consortium bids due in the Autumn (Sep-Nov).

Figure 1: Deadline density by month showing Spring/Autumn activity peaks.

Spring Phase

Focus on Individual & Outline submissions. Critical window for Leverhulme and Nuffield concepts.

Summer Phase

Preparation Period. A convenient time to prepare and negotiate partners for the upcoming Autumn deadlines.

Autumn Phase

Focus on Major Consortia. High-value, complex bids like Horizon Cluster 2 and 3.

Strategic Opportunity Matrix

Mapping the "Big Four" priorities and other key schemes by Urgency (Timeline) and Value. The upper-left quadrant represents immediate, high-value "Flagship" targets that require urgent attention.

Centre Portfolio Alignment

Analysis of how the 25 funding opportunities align with our three priority research centres. Building Future Communities has the highest volume of aligned calls, particularly in social justice (Nuffield, JRF). Digital x Data has high-value strategic targets in cross-council pilots.

Building Future Communities

Strongest alignment with Nuffield (Strategic & Racial Diversity) and JRF. The "nothing about us without us" participatory ethos is a key competitive advantage for these funders.

Digital x Data

Primary target for UKRI Cross-Council Pilot and ESRC Secondary Data Analysis. Unique niche in "politics of computation" fits AHRC digital calls perfectly.

Health & Wellbeing

Laser-focused on Wellcome Discovery Awards (Health Humanities). ESRC Research Grants provide the volume for health inequality work.
